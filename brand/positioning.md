## Positioning Statement

AppVentory is the only SaaS management platform built for advisors and the SMBs they serve — discovering every subscription through accounting data, not enterprise IT infrastructure.

---

## Core Message & Angles

### Angle 1 — The Advisor Platform
Every competitor targets the internal buyer (IT, Finance, Procurement) inside a single organisation. AppVentory owns the external advisor — the accountant, tech advisor, or MSP who manages software on behalf of multiple clients.

This is not a feature difference. It is an audience and workflow difference. AppVentory gives advisors multi-client visibility, client-ready reporting, and structured advisory workflows that turn SaaS management into a repeatable, monetisable service.

### Angle 2 — Accounting-Native Discovery
Competitors rely on SSO and identity provider integrations that SMBs typically don't have. AppVentory connects to the systems SMBs already trust — Xero, QuickBooks, FreshBooks — and surfaces every subscription from financial transaction data. No IT setup. No enterprise infrastructure required.

This makes AppVentory the only tool that genuinely works for SMBs at the accounting layer, and a natural fit for the advisors already operating there.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Advisors
- Accountants, tech advisors, managed service providers (MSPs)
- Already have trusted client relationships; lack the tools to make SaaS management part of what they deliver
- Manage between 5 and 50 client accounts
- Want to add a new service line without adding significant overhead
- Fluent in financial data; not necessarily technical

### Secondary: SMBs (direct)
- Founders, operators, or finance managers at businesses with 10–200 employees
- Do not have a dedicated IT team — the owner or finance lead manages software decisions
- Already using cloud accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, FreshBooks)
- Have heard "you're wasting money on software" before; want to see it proven and acted on

---

## Top 3 Pain Points We Address

### 1. Advisors have no structured way to manage or optimise client software stacks
Current tools assume a single organisation. Advisors working across multiple clients have no centralised view, no client-ready output format, and no workflow to turn SaaS insights into billable recommendations.

### 2. SMBs can't use most SaaS management tools — they're built for enterprises
The standard discovery approach requires SSO, IdP, or IT admin access that most SMBs don't have. The tools that claim SMB support still require setup complexity that small teams can't justify.

### 3. Visibility without action is just a dashboard
Businesses have been promised "25% savings" by every tool in the market. What's missing is a structured path from insight to recommendation to verified outcome — something advisors need to demonstrate ROI to clients.

## Tone of Voice

### Character
Direct. Confident. Grounded in outcomes, not features. Speaks like a trusted advisor — not a tech vendor.

### Principles

**Clear over clever.**
Never sacrifice comprehension for wit. If someone has to read it twice to understand it, rewrite it.

**Lead with the problem, not the product.**
Start from what the advisor or SMB is experiencing. Let the product be the answer, not the opening line.

**Specific over vague.**
"Discover every subscription from your Xero data" beats "gain full visibility into your software stack." Concrete always wins.

**Confident, not arrogant.**
We know this market is saturated with similar claims. We don't shout louder — we say something different.

**Advisor-aware.**
When writing for advisors, acknowledge their world: client relationships, time across multiple accounts, the need to present clearly to non-technical clients. Don't write for an IT manager.

### What to avoid
- Overused SaaS management phrases: "single source of truth", "shadow IT", "eliminate waste", "full visibility"
- Savings percentages without context (the market is numb to 20–30% claims)
- Enterprise jargon (governance, provisioning, deprovisioning) unless in a technical context
- Passive constructions and filler ("helps you to better understand your...")
